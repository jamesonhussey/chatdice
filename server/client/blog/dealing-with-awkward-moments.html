Dealing with Awkward Moments in Online Chat

Published January 2026 ‚Ä¢ 6 min read

Awkwardness happens to everyone in random chat. A joke that lands wrong, an uncomfortable silence, accidentally saying too much. The key isn't avoiding awkwardness. It's knowing how to handle it when it happens.

Awkward Silences

Both of you waiting for the other person to type. The digital version of two people trying to walk through a doorway at the same time.

What to do: Just acknowledge it. "lol okay that got awkward" or "we both paused at the same time huh." Or just change the topic completely. "random question..." works fine. Also remember that silences in text aren't as bad as they feel. The other person might just be thinking or doing something else for a second.

Your Joke Bombs

You say something you think is funny. They respond with "huh?" or just "ok." Cringe.

What to do: Own it. "okay that was funnier in my head lol" and move on. Don't explain the joke. That makes it worse. And don't apologize a bunch. Just treat it as a minor blip and keep going. Everyone has jokes that don't land.

You Overshared

You're in the flow of conversation and suddenly realize you just mentioned your workplace by name or said something way too personal.

What to do: Don't panic. The other person is probably not paying close attention to your specific details. If it's something sensitive, just change the subject. "anyway, enough about me, what about you?" If you're really uncomfortable, you can always leave. But usually it's not as bad as it feels in the moment.

They Say Something Offensive

They drop an opinion or joke that crosses a line. Now you're stuck deciding how to respond.

What to do: You can call it out calmly ("that's not cool"), you can just leave, or you can ask them to clarify. Sometimes asking "wait what do you mean by that?" makes people realize how they sounded. But you don't owe anyone politeness if they're making you uncomfortable. Just leave.

You Don't Get Their Reference

They mention a show or meme or game you've never heard of. Do you pretend to know what they're talking about?

What to do: Just ask. "I don't know that one, what is it?" is totally fine. People usually like explaining things they're into. Pretending to know stuff is obvious and makes you seem fake.

Vibe Mismatch

You're keeping it light but they're getting really intense about something. Or the opposite.

What to do: You can try to match their energy if you're up for it. Or you can be honest: "not really in the headspace for heavy stuff rn, hope that's cool." If it's really not working, it's okay to leave. Not every conversation is meant to work.

Can't Tell If They're Joking

Text doesn't have tone. Sometimes you genuinely can't tell if they're serious or being sarcastic.

What to do: Just ask. "wait are you serious or joking?" is a normal thing to say. Or hedge your response: "if you're serious then..." gives you an out if you read it wrong.

The Conversation is Dead

You've run out of things to say. The energy is gone. But neither of you is leaving.

What to do: Just end it. "I'm gonna head out, this was fun though." There's no shame in conversations ending naturally. Trying to force a dead conversation to continue just makes it worse.

Pacing Issues

You're typing fast, they're slow. Or vice versa. Messages overlapping or weird pauses happening.

What to do: Try to match their pace. If they're slow, slow down. Don't take it personally. People multitask.

General Principles

Acknowledge it when things get awkward. Sometimes just saying "well this is awkward lol" breaks the tension. Use humor. Don't dwell on it. What feels mortifying to you probably barely registers to them. And remember: this is anonymous. Your most embarrassing moment won't follow you.

Sometimes Lean Into It

Sometimes the best move is to embrace the awkwardness instead of trying to fix it. "okay this is weird but in a funny way" can actually create bonding. Some of the best conversations start with awkward moments that both people laugh about.

And if all else fails? There's always the reroll button.
